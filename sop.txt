Coffee beans must be dried to 12% moisture.
Grade A beans require yield above 22%.
Machine speed should remain below 180 RPM.
Roasting temperature must not exceed 220 degrees Celsius.
Grade B beans require yield above 15%.
Storage humidity must be kept below 60% relative humidity.
Beans must be stored at a temperature between 15 and 25 degrees Celsius.
Each batch must be labeled with the harvest date and lot number.
Quality inspection is required every 4 hours during production.
Defective beans must not exceed 3% of total batch weight.
Packaging must occur within 48 hours of roasting.
Machines must be cleaned and sanitized at the start of every shift.
Operators must wear gloves and hairnets at all times on the production floor.
Any batch with moisture above 14% must be rejected and re-dried.
Cooling time after roasting must be at least 20 minutes before packaging.
Conveyor belt speed must not exceed 0.5 meters per second.
All scales must be calibrated weekly and logged in the maintenance sheet.
Bags must be sealed within 10 minutes of filling to preserve freshness.
Any equipment malfunction must be reported to the shift supervisor immediately.
Final products must pass a metal detector scan before shipment.
